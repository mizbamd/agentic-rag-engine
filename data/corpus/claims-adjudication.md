# Healthcare Claims Adjudication

Claims adjudication is the process a payer uses to decide how much of a submitted medical claim to
pay. The claim flows through eligibility verification, benefit determination, pricing against the
contracted fee schedule, and application of member cost-sharing such as deductible and coinsurance.
Each adjudicated claim line is an auditable financial event, much like a ledger posting. When a claim
is later voided or adjusted, a compensating adjustment reverses the original line. HIPAA requires a
complete, reconstructable audit trail of every adjudication decision.
